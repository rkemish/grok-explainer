# Deep AI Stock Due Diligence Brief

Use this template for a fuller investor review of an AI-related stock when you want more than a quick snapshot.

## Input

Prepare a detailed AI investment briefing on `{TICKER}`.

Evaluate whether the company is a credible AI beneficiary, an overhyped AI narrative, or a mixed case.

## Data Sources

### Polygon

Use Polygon for:

- Current price, daily move, volume, market cap, and recent trading range
- Financial metrics, including trailing PE, forward PE, revenue growth, margins, and free cash flow if available
- Historical price performance over 1 week, 1 month, 3 months, year to date, and 1 year
- Options chain highlights, especially near-term unusual call or put activity

### Web Search

Use `web_search` to find the most relevant recent sources about:

- AI-related product launches, partnerships, or infrastructure deals
- Earnings commentary, analyst notes, or management guidance mentioning AI
- Competitive positioning versus other AI-related companies
- Material risks, regulatory issues, customer concentration, or execution concerns

### X Search

Use X search, both keyword and semantic, to assess:

- Current market sentiment around `{TICKER}`
- Whether the AI narrative is gaining or fading
- Breaking rumors, hype cycles, or unusually viral claims
- Credible investor, analyst, or industry commentary from the last 24-72 hours

## Output Format

Write the briefing in the following structure:

1. **Executive View:** One-paragraph summary with a clear bullish, bearish, or mixed stance.
2. **Market Snapshot:** Price, daily move, volume, market cap, and recent performance.
3. **AI Thesis:** Explain how `{TICKER}` is connected to AI and whether that connection is financially meaningful.
4. **Fundamentals:** Summarize valuation, growth, profitability, and balance-sheet context.
5. **Options and Positioning:** Highlight notable calls, puts, implied volatility, and signs of speculative activity.
6. **News and Catalysts:** List the most important recent AI-related developments with dates and sources.
7. **Sentiment Read:** Summarize X and market narrative as bullish, bearish, or mixed.
8. **Key Risks:** Identify the top risks that could break the AI thesis.
9. **What to Watch Next:** List upcoming catalysts, earnings items, product milestones, or technical levels.
10. **Investor Takeaway:** Give a concise risk/reward conclusion and avoid making a guaranteed prediction.

## Quality Rules

- Distinguish confirmed facts from speculation.
- Cite dates and sources for news items.
- Do not rely on stale market data when a live tool can verify it.
- Keep the tone direct, balanced, and useful for an investor.
